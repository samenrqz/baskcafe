Sustainability

Our Environment

Every cup we serve is a small promise to the planet — grown with care, served with intention, and consumed with gratitude.

How We Care

Our Four Green Pillars

Sustainability at Bask isn't a marketing strategy — it's a genuine commitment built into every decision we make.

Ethical Sourcing
We source 100% of our coffee beans from certified ethical farms — paying fair wages to farmers and supporting communities at origin.
Zero-Waste Goals
We are committed to eliminating single-use plastic from all our operations by 2026 — already at 65% replacement with compostable alternatives.
Clean Energy
Our Valenzuela City branch is powered with 40% solar energy. We are planning to reach 80% by end of 2025.
Community Engagement
From barangay clean-ups to scholar sponsorships — we invest in the community that welcomed us.

What We're Doing

Sustainability Initiatives

These aren't just ideas — they're active programs running right now in and around Bask Café.

Coffee Grounds Composting
Instead of throwing away spent coffee grounds, we package and distribute them free to local community gardens. Coffee grounds are an excellent natural fertilizer.
Monthly Barangay Clean-Up
Every first Saturday of the month, Bask staff joins the Marulas barangay in a neighborhood clean-up drive. We bring the coffee — everyone brings the energy.
Bring Your Own Cup Discount
Customers who bring their own reusable cup get ₱10 off any drink. Since launching this in 2023, we've saved over 8,000 disposable cups from landfill.
Tree-for-Every-100-Cups Program
For every 100 cups sold, Bask plants one tree through our partnership with a local environmental NGO. We've planted over 400 trees since 2022.
Compostable Packaging Transition
Our cups, straws, and food packaging are transitioning to 100% compostable materials. We're currently at 65% — with full transition targeted by mid-2026.
Bask Scholar Program
We sponsor 3 scholars annually from Marulas and surrounding barangays — students pursuing food technology, HRM, or agricultural courses.

Community First

Community Engagement

We believe a café's responsibility doesn't stop at its front door — it extends into the neighborhood, the city, and the environment beyond.

Local Supplier Priority

We source ingredients locally whenever possible — from the eggs in our croissants to the bread and sugar in our pastries. Keeping it local keeps the economy circular.

Free Reading Corner

Our café houses a curated free reading corner — open to all. Donate a book, take a book. We partner with barangay libraries to refresh the collection quarterly.

Platform for Local Artists

Our walls rotate local artwork every two months. Artists keep 90% of any sales — because art deserves support as much as coffee deserves good soil.

Food Donation Program

Unsold pastries at closing time are donated to local partner organizations. In 2024, we donated over 2,400 pastry servings — and counting.

Barista Skills Seminar

Twice a year we open free barista skills seminars to the public — helping aspiring café workers in Valenzuela City build real, employable skills.

Water Conservation

We've installed water-saving fixtures and recycle sink water for plant irrigation. Our operational water use is down 30% compared to our 2019 baseline.
